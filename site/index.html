Positive Constraint

Wisdom for your context.

The world is too complex to predict and too fast to adapt to. But not everything changes. Constraints-thinking looks past the noise to what holds still — the forces, truths, and structures that shape everything around them.

These ideas are what that lens reveals. Short, standalone pieces — frameworks, patterns, pitfalls, plays — each one found by asking what doesn't change, and why it matters.
