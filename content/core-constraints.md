# Core Constraints

A **core constraint** is the irreducible truth at the center of a problem domain. It's what remains when you've [[abstraction|abstracted]] away everything non-essential.

Every business has them. Most businesses haven't named them.

---

## Why they matter

Markets change. Technologies shift. Customer preferences evolve. But core constraints are stable — they describe what is *fundamentally true* about a problem, not what happens to be true today.

When you build your business identity around core constraints, you gain:

- **Clarity** — you know what decisions to make and which to skip
- **Resilience** — your strategy doesn't break when the market shifts
- **Narrative power** — you can explain your value in terms that stick

This is the foundation of everything [[positive-constraint|Positive Constraint]] does.

## How they're found

Core constraints don't come from brainstorming. They come from [[abstraction|abstraction]] — from getting close to the problem domain, interviewing stakeholders, and then stepping back far enough to see the shape of the thing.
